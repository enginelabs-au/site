# 01 — Positioning & Brand Vocabulary

## Locked positioning

**Lane C — SMB labour-replacement.**

> Stop hiring for repeatable work. Engineer it instead.

This is the dominant frame. The site speaks to the way SMB owners, operators and lean founders already think: "Do I hire another person, or can I automate this?" Engine Labs is the answer to the second half of that question.

### Why Lane C (and not A or B)

- It matches how non-technical buyers actually phrase the problem. "Hiring is expensive / I can't find people / I'm drowning in admin" is a more visceral pain than "I need an AI workflow."
- It cleanly justifies the price points in the Pricing Schedule. A A$3,500 build that replaces 10 hours/week of admin work is an easy ROI story; a A$3,500 "AI workflow" is an abstract one.
- It naturally narrows the buyer pool to the verticals the contracts can safely serve (SMB ops, agencies, trades, e-comm, recruiters, coaches) and away from the regulated/mission-critical work the MSA excludes.
- The Founder Engine still serves pre-seed founders inside this frame: they're hiring *themselves* less by engineering the work instead.

### What this means in copy

- Lead with verbs of substitution: *replace, retire, engineer, ship, run, hand off.*
- Avoid: *transform, revolutionise, disrupt, harness, leverage, unleash, supercharge, AI-powered.* (Vague, salesy, dated, and over-indexed by every other AI agency.)
- Talk about *tasks* and *roles* the system replaces, not the technology that powers it. "An agent that triages your inbox" not "an LLM-powered inbox classification pipeline."

## Brand vocabulary (locked)

Use these terms consistently across the site, the Control Centre, marketing copy, SOWs and handover docs. Consistency is half of how a single-operator business reads as a company.

| Term | Meaning | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Engine Labs** | The company. | Always two words, capital E, capital L. Never "EngineLabs" or "engine labs". |
| **Engine** | A productized, outcome-named service (Sales Engine, Ops Engine, etc.). | Capitalised when naming a specific Engine. Lowercase when speaking generically ("we build engines for…"). |
| **The Lab** | The workshop frame for everything Engine Labs builds, including build-in-public demos. | Used in `/lab` URL and "from the Lab" content tags. |
| **Control Centre** | The interactive AI tool on the landing page and the logged-in client portal. | Australian spelling (Centre, not Center). One name for both the public and the logged-in view. |
| **Build → Run loop** | The methodology: Intake → Scope → Build → Acceptance → Handover → (optional) Run. | Mirrors the contract pack flow. Use the arrow form `Build → Run` in headings; spell it out in body. |
| **Brief** | A submitted problem statement in the Control Centre. | Replaces "enquiry", "lead", "contact form submission". |
| **Spec sheet** | The published one-pager for each Engine. | Stands in for a case study. |
| **Handover** | The end-of-build delivery package. | Always present-tense and concrete: "you get the handover", not "we hand off". |
| **The Founder Engine** | Specifically the MVP + prototype + roadmap bundle. | Use the definite article. |

### Words to retire (do not use anywhere)

- "Solutions" (use *engine*, *workflow*, *agent*, or the specific outcome).
- "Cutting-edge" / "state-of-the-art" / "next-generation."
- "Bespoke AI partner" / "AI transformation partner."
- "We harness the power of…" / "We leverage…"
- "End-to-end" (overused; if you mean it, say what's at each end).
- "Enterprise-grade" (the contracts explicitly disclaim enterprise scope — using the word in copy creates contradiction).

### Words to keep (Australian voice)

- Spellings: *centre, organisation, optimise, customise, behaviour, programme* (for a series), *labour*.
- Currency: always `A$` prefix (matches the Pricing Schedule).
- Tone: direct, plain, operator-grade. Short sentences. Concrete nouns. No hype. The voice in the contract pack ("practical product/operator builder") is the voice of the site.

## Tagline candidates

In priority order. First two are for hero use; rest are for social, email, supporting pages.

1. **Stop hiring for repeatable work. Engineer it instead.** (Hero. Lane C distilled.)
2. **An agentic company, building agentic companies.** (Founding line. Keep in the about section / footer.)
3. **You brief it. We build it. It runs itself.** (Three-beat. Good for sub-hero or social.)
4. **The engine room for AI-powered businesses.** (Fallback / about-page descriptor.)
5. **Hire the work, not the person.** (Lane C, shorter. Good for ads / social.)

## One-paragraph "about" boilerplate

For footers, social bios, email signatures, partner intros:

> Engine Labs is a one-operator AI build studio. We design and ship Engines — small, productized AI workflows, agents and internal tools — that retire repeatable work inside small businesses, agencies and founder-led teams. Every build comes with a fixed scope, a fixed price, a published spec sheet and a clean handover. No retainers required, no enterprise theatre, no promises we can't back up in a contract.

## Voice rules in five lines

1. Sound like an operator, not a vendor.
2. Show pricing. Show exclusions. Show the methodology.
3. Replace adjectives with examples.
4. Never promise a business outcome the contracts disclaim (revenue, leads, uptime, accuracy).
5. If the contract pack wouldn't sign it, the landing page doesn't say it.
